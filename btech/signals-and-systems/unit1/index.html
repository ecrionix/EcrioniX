1. What is a Signal?

A signal is any physical quantity that is a function of one or more independent variables and carries information. In ECE, signals are typically functions of time.

Examples: voltage across a resistor v(t), audio waveform x(t), ECG trace, digital data stream, image intensity I(x,y).

Key Distinction

Continuous-Time (CT) signal ‚ÄĒ defined for all values of t ‚ąą (‚ąí‚ąě, ‚ąě). Example: sinusoidal voltage from an oscillator.
Discrete-Time (DT) signal ‚ÄĒ defined only at integer values of n. Example: digital audio samples x[n] at 44100 Hz sampling rate.

Signal Classification Tree ‚ÄĒ Overview

Every signal is either CT or DT; independently, either periodic or aperiodic; and separately classified as an energy signal or power signal (but not both ‚ÄĒ except the trivial x=0 case).

2.4 Deterministic vs Random

Deterministic signals are fully described by a mathematical expression ‚ÄĒ no uncertainty. Random signals are described statistically (mean, variance, power spectral density). In this unit, we deal with deterministic signals only.
