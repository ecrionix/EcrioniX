CELLULAR RESPIRATION

Glycolysis & The Krebs Cycle

The Powerhouse of Life

Cellular respiration is the metabolic process by which cells obtain energy in the form of Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP). It involves the catabolism (breakdown) of glucose ($C_6H_{12}O_6$) in the presence of oxygen ($O_2$). The overall chemical equation is:

\[ C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 \rightarrow 6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{Energy (ATP)} \]

1. Glycolysis: "Sugar Splitting"

Location: Cytoplasm (outside the mitochondria).
Requirement: Anaerobic (Does not require Oxygen).

Glycolysis is the first stage of respiration. It takes one molecule of Glucose (a 6-carbon ring) and breaks it down into two molecules of Pyruvate (3-carbon chains).

  • Energy Investment Phase: The cell actually spends 2 ATP molecules to phosphorylate the glucose, making it unstable and ready to split.
  • Energy Payoff Phase: The split molecules are oxidized to create Pyruvate. In this process, 4 ATP are produced (net gain of 2 ATP) and 2 molecules of $NAD^+$ are reduced to NADH (electron carriers).

2. The Link Reaction (Pyruvate Oxidation)

Location: Mitochondrial Matrix.
Before entering the Krebs Cycle, the Pyruvate must be groomed. It loses a carbon atom (released as $CO_2$) and combines with Coenzyme A to form Acetyl-CoA. This also generates more NADH.

3. The Krebs Cycle (Citric Acid Cycle)

Location: Mitochondrial Matrix.
Requirement: Aerobic (Requires Oxygen indirectly).

This is a metabolic furnace that harvests high-energy electrons. Acetyl-CoA (2 carbons) merges with Oxaloacetate (4 carbons) to form **Citrate** (6 carbons). Through a series of 8 enzymatic steps, the citrate is oxidized back into oxaloacetate, ready to begin the cycle again.

Outputs per Glucose Molecule (2 turns of the cycle):

  • 4 $CO_2$: Waste product exhaled by the organism.
  • 2 ATP: Directly usable energy (via GTP).
  • 6 NADH: High-energy electron carriers.
  • 2 $FADH_2$: Another type of electron carrier.

4. The Electron Transport Chain (ETC)

Location: Inner Mitochondrial Membrane (Cristae).

This is the final payoff phase. The NADH and $FADH_2$ produced in the previous steps drop off their high-energy electrons here. As electrons move down the chain, they pump protons ($H^+$) into the intermembrane space, creating a gradient.

Chemiosmosis: The protons flow back into the matrix through a turbine-like enzyme called ATP Synthase, which generates the vast majority of the ATP (approx 30-32 ATP per glucose). Oxygen sits at the end of the chain to catch the spent electrons and protons, forming Water ($H_2O$).
