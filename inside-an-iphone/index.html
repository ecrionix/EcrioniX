One iPhone, a planet-spanning supply chain

It's easy to think of an iPhone as "an Apple product." In reality, Apple designs the phone and a handful of its chips — but the device is assembled from parts made by dozens of specialist companies across at least seven countries. No single company, not even Apple, could build an iPhone alone. That interdependence is the most important fact about the modern semiconductor industry.

The clearest example is the brain of the phone. The Apple A-series processor (e.g. the A17 Pro) is designed by Apple in California — but Apple owns no factories. It is a fabless company that relies entirely on TSMC in Taiwan to manufacture those chips on the world's most advanced 3nm process. One design house, one foundry, two countries — for just one part.

Why is it so global?

Because no one is best at everything. Decades of specialization mean each step is dominated by a few experts: TSMC leads cutting-edge fabrication, Sony leads image sensors, Samsung and LG lead OLED, Qualcomm leads modems, Largan leads precision optics. Apple's genius is orchestrating these world-class suppliers into one product — and using its scale to fund the very R&D that keeps them ahead.

This also explains why chip supply chains are now a geopolitical issue. A single device depends on Taiwan, South Korea, Japan, the US, the Netherlands and China simultaneously — which is exactly why the semiconductor boom, export controls, and new fabs in the US and elsewhere make headlines. (Apple is also gradually bringing more in-house, like its own silicon and a first-generation modem.)

FAQ

Who makes the iPhone processor?

Apple designs it; TSMC in Taiwan manufactures it (currently on a 3nm process). Apple is fabless — it owns no chip factories.

Who supplies the camera and display?

Image sensors from Sony (Japan), lenses from Largan (Taiwan); OLED panels from Samsung & LG (Korea), with some BOE (China).

Who makes the 5G modem?

Mostly Qualcomm (Snapdragon X70/X71). Apple introduced its own C1 modem in the iPhone 16e in 2025.

Is the iPhone made by one company?

No — Apple designs it, but parts come from dozens of companies across seven-plus countries, assembled mainly by Foxconn.
