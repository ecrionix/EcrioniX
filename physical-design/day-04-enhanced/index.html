Standard Cell Placement

Placement strategies, congestion analysis, timing-driven placement, power optimization, and production verification for VLSI chips.

By EcrioniX · Published June 14, 2026 · ~4300 words · 13 min read

1. Placement Fundamentals

Placement determines physical locations of standard cells (logic gates). Good placement minimizes wire length, congestion, and power while enabling timing closure.

Placement objectives:

  • Minimize total wire length (reduces power and delay)
  • Reduce congestion (avoid routing bottlenecks)
  • Meet timing constraints (critical path closure)
  • Optimize power distribution (minimize IR drop)
  • Facilitate manufacturability (avoid hotspots, lithography issues)

2. Placement Algorithms

Global Placement

Coarse-level positioning of all cells. Uses quadratic optimization or simulated annealing to find general location of each cell.

  • Time: Seconds to minutes
  • Output: Rough initial positions (not legal)
  • Next step: Detailed placement for final positioning

Detailed Placement

Fine-tuning positions and resolving overlaps. Ensures no cell overlaps, respects white-space usage.

  • Time: Minutes to hours (depends on size)
  • Output: Legal placement (no overlaps, within core area)
  • Iterative: Works with routing to optimize globally

3. Congestion Analysis

Congestion predicts routing difficulty. High congestion → timing closure challenges.

Congestion metrics: Density = (cell area + routing area) / total area Low density: <70% → plenty of routing space High density: >80% → tight routing, hard to close Local congestion (Gcell-based): Gcells = divide die into 1mm × 1mm tiles Measure demand vs supply for each Gcell Red zones: demand > supply (need rerouting) Blue zones: demand < supply (over-provisioned) Congestion hotspots (typical issues): - Large macros surrounded by many connections - Core logic clustered too tightly - Poor macro placement forcing long routes

4. Timing-Driven Placement

Move cells to meet timing constraints. Critical paths get priority placement (shorter wire length).

  • Critical cell clustering: Place cells on same critical path close together
  • Slew-driven placement: Reduce interconnect capacitance for timing paths
  • Buffer insertion: Add buffers on long wires to meet slew/delay targets

5. Power-Aware Placement

  • Thermal awareness: Spread power-hungry blocks (avoid hotspots)
  • Leakage power: Group cells by voltage domain (multiple Vdd)
  • Switching power: Minimize interconnect length (capacitive coupling)

6. Real-World Placement Examples

7nm Processor

  • 100 mm² die, 75% utilization
  • 50M standard cells
  • Global placement: 2 minutes
  • Detailed placement: 30 minutes
  • Timing closure: 95% paths met after placement

5nm GPU

  • 500 mm² die, 80% utilization (aggressive)
  • 500M+ standard cells
  • Hierarchical placement (place sub-blocks first)
  • Timing closure harder (tight skew budget, high power density)

7. Placement Checklist

  • ✅ Define placement constraints: Critical paths, thermal limits
  • ✅ Run global placement: Ensure reasonable initial positions
  • ✅ Analyze congestion: Identify hotspots early
  • ✅ Timing-driven detailed placement: Optimize critical paths
  • ✅ Power analysis: Check thermal distribution
  • ✅ DFM awareness: Avoid manufacturability issues
  • ✅ Verify legality: No overlaps, within core area
  • ✅ Incremental placement: Post-routing optimization if needed
