1. What is Metastability?

In a perfect world, a digital signal is either a 0 or a 1. In reality, signals are voltages. When a Flip-Flop (FF) captures data, it needs a tiny bit of time to "decide" and settle.

"Metastability is a state where a digital signal hovers in an undefined voltage range, neither high nor low, for an indeterminate amount of time."

Imagine a ball perched perfectly on top of a sharp hill. If you don't push it hard enough to one side, it might wobble at the peak for a long time before finally falling. That "wobble" is metastability.
