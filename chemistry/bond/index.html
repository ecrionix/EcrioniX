Chemical Bonding

Atoms are rarely found in isolation. Instead, they seek stability by achieving a full outer electron shell (usually 8 electrons, known as the Octet Rule). This stability is achieved through chemical bonds.

1. Ionic Bonding

Transfer of Electrons: Occurs typically between a Metal and a Non-metal.

  • The metal atom loses electrons to become a positive cation.
  • The non-metal gains electrons to become a negative anion.
  • The resulting electrostatic attraction between opposite charges holds the lattice together.

Example: \(Na + Cl \rightarrow Na^+Cl^-\) (Table Salt)

2. Covalent Bonding

Sharing of Electrons: Occurs typically between Non-metals.

  • Atoms "overlap" their valence shells so that electrons count toward the octet of both atoms.
  • This sharing can be equal (non-polar) or unequal (polar) depending on electronegativity.

Example: \(H + H \rightarrow H_2\) (Hydrogen Gas)
